Giving Feedback
Strong Teams: Feedback, Communication & Performance Improvement

Name: Class: Due date:

What You Need To Do

You will create a Google Slides folio that shows how you used feedback to improve movement skills and teamwork across the unit. Your folio is like a digital coaching portfolio. It should show what you noticed, what feedback you gave or received, and how performance improved your control, accuracy, communication and team performance.

Why Feedback Matters

Feedback helps people improve because it gives them clear information about what worked and what to try next. In strong teams, feedback is respectful, specific and useful. Positive communication makes games more enjoyable and more successful because teammates stay calm, keep trying and help each other improve.

Team Culture Matters

Behaviours that hurt team performance:
  • yelling after mistakes
  • blaming teammates
  • sarcasm or put-downs
  • refusing to pass or include others
  • arguing over calls
  • negative body language
Behaviours that improve team performance:
  • encouragement
  • calm communication
  • helpful coaching cues
  • helping teammates reset
  • effort after mistakes
  • staying positive and inclusive

Your Google Slides Folio

Your folio must include:
  • Annotated skill slides from the focus sport or modified games used in class.
  • Key coaching points, movement ideas and common mistakes.
  • Helpful feedback examples, including what you saw, heard or felt while improving.
  • Peer feedback records.
  • Short reflections about your performance, communication, emotions and teamwork.
  • A final performance improvement reflection that explains what changed and why.
