Giving Feedback
Strong Teams: Feedback, Communication & Performance Improvement

What This Unit Is About

In this unit, you will focus on one main sport for several lessons. You will build skills step by step, use feedback to improve, and then apply those skills in small games and simple tactics. You will also learn how positive communication makes games more enjoyable and more successful.

You will practise:
  • core skills from the focus sport
  • control, accuracy and decision-making
  • moving into space and supporting teammates
  • simple attacking and defending tactics
  • teamwork and communication
You will build:
  • a Google Slides folio
  • skill annotations
  • peer feedback records
  • short reflections
  • a final performance improvement reflection

Good Feedback Sounds Like

  • "One thing you did well was..."
  • "One thing to try next is..."
  • "This could help because..."
  • "The feedback helped me because..."

What Strong Teammates Do

  • Speak respectfully, even when someone makes a mistake.
  • Include people with different skill levels.
  • Listen to feedback and try again.
  • Encourage teammates and help the group stay positive.
  • Use calm communication to improve team performance.
  • Play fairly and make safe decisions.

Helpful And Unhelpful Behaviours

Hurts the team:
  • blaming or yelling
  • arguing over calls
  • sarcasm or negative body language
  • refusing to include others
Helps the team:
  • encouragement
  • helping someone reset
  • specific coaching cues
  • respectful disagreement

Student Quick Checklist

  • I can give one strength and one next step.
  • I can explain a coaching cue clearly.
  • I can use feedback to improve control or accuracy.
  • I can reflect on how I responded to mistakes.
  • I can help my team communicate positively.
