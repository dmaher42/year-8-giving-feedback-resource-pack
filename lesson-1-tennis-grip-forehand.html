Lesson 1: Tennis Grip and Forehand Shot

Unit: Giving Feedback - Strong Teams: Feedback, Communication & Performance Improvement

Learning Intentions

  • We are learning to use a suitable tennis grip and forehand technique.
  • We are learning to explain key coaching points and movement ideas.
  • We are learning to use observation and respectful feedback to improve control and accuracy.

Success Criteria

  • I can show a handshake grip and ready position.
  • I can make contact in front and follow through.
  • I can name one strength and one next step for a partner.
  • I can explain whether feedback was visual, auditory or kinaesthetic.
  • I can respond to feedback calmly and try it again.

Materials

Tennis racquets, tennis balls or soft balls, cones, printed peer feedback sheets, whiteboard or display, Google Slides folio access.

Teacher Script

"Today we are starting our unit on giving feedback. Feedback is not about judging people. It is information that helps someone know what to keep doing and what to try next. In tennis, we will focus on grip, ready position, contact point, balance and follow through. Your job is to observe carefully, practise the movement and coach your partner using respectful, specific language. We will also notice whether feedback is visual, auditory or kinaesthetic: what you see, what you hear, or what you feel in your movement."

Lesson Flow

0-5 minEntry and focusIntroduce unit title, learning intention and feedback sentence starters.
5-10 minWarm-upMovement warm-up with side steps, split steps, reaction calls and partner ball rolls.
10-18 minExplicit teachingDemonstrate handshake grip, ready position, side-on stance, racquet back early, contact in front, follow through and balanced finish.
18-35 minPractical activityPartner feed and forehand: one student drop-feeds or gently tosses, one hits to a target zone, then swap. Coach gives one strength and one cue.
35-45 minChallenge roundStudents aim for a target area. Emphasise calm reset after misses: breathe, listen, try one cue.
45-55 minClassroom slideshow taskStart folio slides 1-4. Students add personal goal, strong teammate ideas, forehand annotations and first feedback reflection.
55-60 minReflectionExit prompt: "What feedback helped you improve today? What will you try next lesson?"

Behaviour and Communication Focus

  • Use names and calm voices when coaching.
  • Start feedback with a strength before a next step.
  • Respond to mistakes with encouragement, not blame.
  • Include partners by adjusting feed speed, distance or ball type.
  • Use the reset phrase: "Good try. One thing to try next is..."

Teamwork Bonus Scoring

Use this during partner challenges or small games so students see that communication affects success.

  • +1 for encouragement after a mistake.
  • +1 for helping a teammate reset calmly.
  • +1 for a useful coaching cue.
  • +1 for positive leadership or including someone.
  • +1 for respectful disagreement about a call.

Teacher prompt: "Today, success is not just how many points you win. It is also how your communication helps your partner improve."
