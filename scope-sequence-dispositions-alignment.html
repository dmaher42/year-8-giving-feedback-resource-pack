Unit: Giving Feedback - Strong Teams: Feedback, Communication & Performance Improvement

Source used: Year 8 Health & Physical Education Scope and Sequence document placed in the Year 8 folder, including Year 7-8 achievement standards, Year 8 Movement and Physical Activity rows, and Year 8 Personal, Social and Community Health rows.

Year 7-8 Achievement Standard Alignment

Achievement standard focusWhere it appears in this unitEvidence collected
Apply and transfer movement skills and movement concepts across a range of situations.Students usually work within one main focus sport long enough to build connected skills, then apply those skills in modified games and tactical situations. Other sports can be used as comparison or transfer examples.Annotated skill slides, practical participation, teacher observation.
Implement and evaluate movement strategies and their effect on outcomes.Students use peer feedback, observation and repeated practice to test whether a cue or strategy improves control, accuracy or team performance.Feedback reflections, final performance improvement reflection.
Select, use and refine strategies that support inclusion, fair play and collaboration.Students practise respectful feedback, inclusive team roles, calm responses to mistakes and simple game modifications for safety and participation.Peer feedback sheet, teamwork reflection, teacher checklist.

Scope and Sequence Alignment

Year 8 HPE focusUnit alignmentResource/s
Moving our body: Use feedback to improve control and accuracy of specialist movement skills.Students observe performance, give coaching cues and apply feedback to improve forehand, passing, shooting and game skills.Google Slides folio, peer feedback sheet and annotated examples.
Moving our body: Use ICT or observation methods to analyse own and others' performance.The digital folio requires visual annotation, key coaching points, common mistakes and reflections based on observation.Google Slides folio guide, assessment rubric.
Moving our body: Investigate visual, auditory and kinaesthetic feedback.Students compare what they see, hear and feel when improving a skill and identify which feedback type helped most.Folio reflections, exit slips, teacher questioning.
Movement concepts: Apply and transfer strategies in different movement situations.Students progress from individual skill technique into tactical ideas such as space, timing, control, support, target accuracy, attacking roles and defending roles.Teacher overview, folio slides, practical observation.
Understanding movement: Explain how elements of movement enhance performance.Students explain contact point, balance, force, timing, follow through, space and relationship to teammates.Annotated forehand and soccer pass examples, student annotations.
Learning through movement: Promote safety, fair play, inclusivity, participation and effort.Students practise positive team behaviours and reflect on how communication, encouragement and behaviour affect enjoyment, inclusion and team performance.Teacher checklist, teamwork reflection, exit slips.
Learning through movement: Use problem-solving and informed decisions to enhance safety and wellbeing.Students use if/then coaching decisions, adjust activities and consider player responsibilities during team games.Practical activities, teacher observation and final reflection.
Communicating and interacting: Maintain respectful relationships on the sports field.Students use sentence starters, listen to partners and give feedback that supports confidence and wellbeing.Peer feedback sheet, student assessment task, rubric.
Communicating and interacting: Analyse emotional responses and demonstrate empathy.Students reflect on frustration, mistakes, confidence and how feedback affects others.Exit slips, teamwork reflection, teacher observation.

SA Education Dispositions Alignment

DispositionExample within unitWhat the teacher can look for
RespectfulCommunicating positively and supporting teammates.Uses calm language, listens, avoids blame and recognises effort.
ReflectiveAnalysing performance and communication.Explains what improved, what feedback helped and what to try next.
CollaborativeWorking together in games and peer coaching.Shares roles, includes others and helps the team solve movement challenges.
ResilientResponding positively to mistakes and feedback.Resets after errors, keeps trying and uses feedback rather than rejecting it.
EmpatheticUnderstanding how feedback affects others.Adapts tone, chooses helpful words and supports peers with different skill levels.
ResponsibleManaging behaviour and contributing to team culture.Uses equipment safely, follows agreed rules and contributes to fair play and positive team culture.
ConfidentDeveloping leadership and movement competence.Explains coaching cues, attempts skills and leads positively when appropriate.
