Google Slides Folio Guide

Unit 4: Giving Feedback
Use this guide to build your Performance Improvement Analysis folio. Each slide should be clear, readable and connected to what you practised in HPE lessons. Your folio should show how feedback improved control, accuracy, movement ideas, communication and team performance.

Slide-By-Slide Instructions

Set Up Your Goal

Slide 1: Unit Title and Personal Goal

Add the unit title, your name and one personal improvement goal.

Title and goal space

Sentence starter: "This unit I want to improve..."

Success criteria: Goal is specific, realistic and connected to movement or teamwork.

Slide 2: What Makes a Strong Teammate?

Explain 4-5 behaviours that help a team perform well and make games more enjoyable.

Icons, words or examples

"A strong teammate shows..."

Success criteria: Includes respect, encouragement, listening, fair play and helping others after mistakes.

Skill Annotation Slides

Slide 3: Focus Sport Skill 1 Annotation

Add or draw the first key skill from the focus sport and label the technique points.

Image or drawing with arrows

"A key coaching point is..."

Success criteria: Labels body position, contact point or release point, balance, timing and follow through where relevant.

Slide 4: Skill 1 Feedback Reflection

Record feedback you received or gave about skill 1. Explain whether it was visual feedback, auditory feedback or kinaesthetic feedback.

Feedback record

"One thing I improved was..."

Success criteria: Feedback is specific and explains how it helped control, accuracy or confidence.

Slide 5: Focus Sport Skill 2 Annotation

Add or draw a second connected skill from the focus sport. Label the key coaching points and common mistakes.

Image or drawing with arrows

"The next skill connects because..."

Success criteria: Explains how this skill supports game performance.

Slide 6: Skill 2 Feedback Reflection

Explain one strength and one next step for skill 2. Include how feedback affected accuracy, force, timing, space or control.

Reflection notes

"One thing to try next is..."

Success criteria: Links the next step to improved accuracy, control or decision-making.

Slide 7: Focus Sport Skill 3 or Tactical Skill Annotation

Choose a third skill or a tactical skill, such as creating space, supporting a teammate, defending, attacking or choosing when to pass/shoot/hit.

Skill or tactic diagram

"A common mistake is..."

Success criteria: Includes body position, timing, decision-making and how it helps the team.

Slide 8: Skill 3 or Tactic Feedback Reflection

Describe feedback from a partner and how you used it in a game or modified activity.

Before and after notes

"The feedback helped me because..."

Success criteria: Shows you received feedback respectfully and tried to apply it.

Slide 9: Modified Game or Tactical Decision Annotation

Annotate one game moment or modified activity. Show how a skill, tactic or communication choice affected the play.

Game moment diagram

"This decision was important because..."

Success criteria: Explains when the skill or tactic is used in a game situation.

Feedback and Reflection Slides

Slide 10: Peer Feedback Record

Add a clear record of feedback you gave and received.

Strengths and next step

"One thing you did well was..."

Success criteria: Uses respectful, specific and helpful language.

Slide 11: Teamwork and Communication Reflection

Reflect on how communication affected the activity. Explain how encouragement, behaviour or calm communication improved team performance.

Teamwork reflection

"Our team worked better when..."

Success criteria: Mentions inclusion, fair play, emotional regulation, encouragement or a modification that helped everyone participate.

Slide 12: Final Performance Improvement Reflection

Explain what improved across the unit and what helped most. Include one example of movement improvement and one example of how positive communication made the activity more successful.

Final reflection

"The most useful feedback I received was..."

Success criteria: Uses examples from practical lessons, evaluates what worked and identifies a future goal.

Tip: Keep slides readable. Use large headings, short dot points and clear labels. Visual feedback means what you see, auditory feedback means what you hear, and kinaesthetic feedback means what you feel in your movement.
